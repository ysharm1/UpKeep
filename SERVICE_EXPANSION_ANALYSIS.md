## The Risks of Adding Too Early

### 1. Confuses Your Message
**Focused:** "UpKeep - AI diagnosis for home repairs"
**Unfocused:** "UpKeep - Home services marketplace"

Which is more compelling? The focused one.

### 2. Harder to Market
- Indoor repairs: Target homeowners with problems
- Lawn care: Target homeowners who want maintenance
- Different customer mindset = different marketing

### 3. Quality Control Issues
- More categories = more providers to vet
- Different skill sets = different verification process
- Harder to maintain 4.5+ rating across all categories

### 4. Operational Complexity
- Different pricing models
- Different scheduling (weekly vs one-time)
- Different customer expectations
- More support tickets

---

## What to Do Instead

### Focus on Depth, Not Breadth

**Instead of adding lawn care, add:**

1. **Better AI Diagnosis**
   - More accurate problem identification
   - Better DIY recommendations
   - Image recognition

2. **Preventive Maintenance**
   - "Your AC needs service every 6 months"
   - Automatic reminders
   - Subscription plans for regular maintenance

3. **Emergency Services**
   - 24/7 emergency dispatch
   - Premium pricing for urgent issues
   - Faster response times

4. **Warranty & Insurance**
   - Job guarantees
   - Extended warranties
   - Insurance partnerships

5. **Home Health Score**
   - Overall home condition assessment
   - Predictive maintenance alerts
   - Home value impact

These add value to your CORE service without diluting focus.

- Accept that you're building a broader marketplace
- Be prepared for more complexity

**But if you're in a medium/large city (>100K people):**
